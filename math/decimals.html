Convert decimals to fraction

Question - What is the equivalent fraction of 0.2?

Decimals are special fractions whose denominators are a power of 10. A power of 10 means 10 multiplied one or more times with itself, like
  • 10
  • 10 $times$ 10
  • 10 $times$ 10 $times$ 10
So 10, 100 and 1000 etc. are powers of 10. To find the equivalent fraction,
  • Find the number of digits after the dot;
  • We have one digit after the dot; in 0.2
  • Hence denominator would be 1 followed by one zero, i.e. 10
Answer is $frac{2}{10}$

Simplify fractions

Big numbers can be written as product of small numbers. Product is just a fancy name for multiplication. All numbers are products of prime numbers. Please check if the number is divisible by prime numbers 2, 3, 5, 7 11, 13 etc. For example,

  • 10 = 2 x 5
  • 30 = 2 x 15 , or 2 x 3 x 5
  • 100 = 2 x 50 or 2 x 2 x 25 or 2 x 2 x 5 x 5
so $frac{30}{100}$ can be expanded to $frac{2 times 3 times 5}{2 times 2 times 5 times 5}$
The common numbers in the numerator and denominator can be cancelled. Here we can cancel the numbers 2 and 5. That gives us,
$frac{cancel(2) times 3 times cancel(5)}{cancel(2) times 2 times cancel(5) times 5}$ = $frac{3}{2 times 5}$ = $frac{3}{10}$

Fractions involving powers of 10

30 and 100 are each multiple of 10, 30 = $3 times 10$ and 100 = $10 times 10$
$frac{30}{100}$ = $frac{ 3 times 10 } {10 times 10}$
10 can be cancelled from both the Numerator and the denominator.
$frac{30}{100}$ = $frac{ 3 times cancel(10) } {10 times cancel(10)}$ = $frac{3}{10}$

The fraction having numbers with zeros can be simplified by inspection.

  • $frac{300}{450}$ = $frac{30 cancel(0)} {45 cancel(0)}$
  • $frac{70}{200}$ = $frac{7 cancel(0)} {20 cancel(0)}$
  • $frac{1500}{2000}$ = $frac{15 cancel(0)cancel(0)} {20 cancel(0)cancel(0)}$
Remember, this only works for whole numbers.

Whole numbers are also decimals

  • 5 is same as 5.000
  • 15 is same as 15.00
  • However 15 $ne$ 150

Question - Where can we add zeros without changing the number?

Left boundary
Start reading from the left, left boundary is where we hit a non-zero digit or dot
Right boundary
Right boundary is at the last nonzero digit after the dot or the dot if it is only followed by zeros.

Examples

  • 0.00 $abs(3205)$ 000

  • 000$abs(132.0004)$0

Multiply decimals by powers of 10

- Multiply a decimal by 10 and the dot moves to the right
- The dot moves for all powers of 10! All we need to do is counting!

  • 12.3456 x 10 = 123.456 ($times 10$ and the dot moves one place to the right)
  • 12.3456 x 1000 = 12345.6 ($times 1000$ and the dot moves three places to the right)

Divide decimals by powers of 10

- Divide a decimal by 10 and the dot moves to the left
- The dot moves for all powers of 10! All we need to do is counting!
  • 12.3456 $div$ 10 = 1.23456 ($times frac{1}{10}$ and the dot moves one place to the left)
  • 1234.5678 $div$ 1000 = 1.2345678 ($times frac{1}{1000}$ and the dot moves three places to the left)

Decimal division

Divisor is a power of 10

It is super easy to do the decimal divison with numbers like 10, 100 and 1000 that are power of 10. These numbers have 1 followed by one or more zeros. All you have to do is to move the dot to the left!!
  • 12.3456 $div$ 10 = 1.23456 ($times frac{1}{10}$ and the dot moves one place to the left)
  • 1234.5678 $div$ 1000 = 1.2345678 ($times frac{1}{1000}$ and the dot moves three places to the left)

Long division for decimals

Before starting, please remember that,
  1. 3 is same as 3.0
  2. Multiplying by 10 will shift the dot to the right
The rules are
  1. Make the divisor a whole number by moving the dot (multiplying)
  2. Move the dot the same number of places in dividend
  3. Mark the dot (very very imp)
  4. write zero if division cannot be performed, try 0.40026 $div$ 0.04 and 2.001 $div$ 0.4

9.3 $div$ 3.1

  1. Make the divisor, 3.1 a whole number by moving the dot to right, to make it 31
  2. Move the dot the same number of places in dividend, 9.3 becomes 93
$root(31)(93)$

195.3 $div$ 0.63

  1. Make the divisor, 0.63 a whole number by moving the dot to right, to make it 63
  2. Move the dot the same number of places in dividend, 195.3 becomes 19530
$root(63)(19530)$

6 $div$ 0.4

  1. Make the divisor, 0.4 a whole number by moving the dot to right, to make it 4
  2. Move the dot the same number of places in dividend, 6
  3. Now remeber, 6 is also 6.00, do not be stuck on while working on whole numbers
  4. So the dividend becomes 60.00
$root(4)(60.000)$
